## OroLLM 

OroLLM is a research-driven initiative to develop efficient Large Language Models for Afaan Oromoo and other underrepresented African languages, enabling NLP applications for education, public services, and digital inclusion in low-resource settings.

##  Project Summary

OroLLM is an academic research initiative led by experts at Adama Science and Technology University (ASTU) aimed at developing scalable and resource-efficient Large Language Models (LLMs) for Afaan Oromoo.

The project addresses the critical gap in Natural Language Processing (NLP) for low-resource African languages, where limited datasets and computational constraints have hindered the development of high-quality language technologies.

## Core Objectives
Develop a foundational Afaan Oromoo language model (OroLLM) optimized for low-resource training environments (≤12GB GPU)
Construct and curate high-quality Afaan Oromoo corpora from diverse domains (education, governance, web text, and conversational data)
Build tokenization and preprocessing pipelines tailored to Afaan Oromoo linguistic structure
Enable downstream NLP applications including:
Machine translation (Afaan Oromoo ↔ English and other regional languages)
Text summarization
Conversational AI for public service delivery
OroLLM is designed to support Ethiopia’s and Africa’s digital transformation goals by enabling:
E-Government services in Afaan Oromoo for improved accessibility and citizen engagement
Educational tools for students and teachers in local languages
Agricultural information systems powered by natural language interfaces
Reduction of the digital language divide affecting underrepresented linguistic communities
## Technical Approach
The project focuses on:
Lightweight and efficient transformer-based architectures
Training strategies optimized for limited GPU resources (12GB local infrastructure)
Modular and reproducible training pipelines
Incremental scaling from baseline models to advanced fine-tuned systems
Open-source development to ensure transparency and reproducibility
## Institutional Context
Lead Institution: Adama Science and Technology University (ASTU), Ethiopia
Research Domain: Natural Language Processing (NLP), Large Language Models, Low-Resource AI
Deployment Vision: Academic research → Open-source models → Public service integration
## Project Status
